“Perhaps travel cannot prevent bigotry, but by demonstrating that all peoples cry, laugh, eat, worry, and die, it can introduce the idea that if we try and understand each other, we may even become friends.” – Maya Angelou

What was most significant about the lunar voyage was not that man set foot on the Moon but that they set eye on the earth.

A Chinese tale tells of some men sent to harm a young girl who, upon seeing her beauty, become her protectors rather than her violators. That's how I felt seeing the Earth for the first time. I could not help but love and cherish her.

For those who have seen the Earth from space, and for the hundreds and perhaps thousands more who will, the experience most certainly changes your perspective. The things that we share in our world are far more valuable than those which divide us.

Spain and Switzerland

I spent the summer after High School traveling for a few months through Spain and Switzerland. I stayed on Mallorca, a small island near Ibizia. My best friend in High School was a foreign exchange student from Spain so I had the opportunity to go stay with her family.

My Dad served a mission for our church for two years in Geneva Switzerland. Before I went off to Spain on my own, I spent a month hiking through the Swiss and Italian alps and it was the best summer ever. The photo down below is from that trip.
